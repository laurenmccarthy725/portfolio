Reporting & Dashboards

A dashboard is only as useful as the decision it enables, and that's something I believe deeply.

The work here spans the full business, from weekly snapshots for cross-functional teams to executive-level reporting reviewed by our corporate parent. Each one required more than knowing which metrics to pull; it meant understanding what each stakeholder was actually trying to accomplish and building something they'd trust enough to act on — including stakeholders for whom data wasn't a native language.

The screenshots show the output, with metrics blurred to preserve confidentiality. What they don't show is the data architecture behind them, the conversations that shaped them, or the moment a number in one of these reports changed the direction of a campaign, a budget, or a strategy. That's the part I care most about.

Tracks cross-platform engagement metrics including impressions, reach, click-through rate, and follower growth across Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and Pinterest. KPIs are segmented by platform and content type to surface top-performing post formats and optimal publishing windows.

Monitors scan volume, scan-to-conversion rates, and geographic distribution across consumer and professional QR code programs spanning packaging, digital, tradeshow, and internal initiatives. Segmented reporting by code type and deployment channel enables performance benchmarking across campaigns and informs placement optimization strategy.

Aggregates ratings, review volume, and sentiment scores across multiple channels to provide a unified view of product perception. Channel-level breakdowns identify where positive and negative feedback concentrates, while trend analysis tracks shifts in average rating and reviews over time.

Tracks in-chair Waterpik recommendation activity across enrolled dental professionals, monitoring program participation rates, recommendation volume, and patient conversion metrics. Engagement trends by practice and region surface top-performing advocates and identify opportunities to expand program adoption within the dental professional network.

Summarizes order volume, revenue, and sales trends across three brands within the dental professional ordering platform. SKU-level and brand-level performance metrics enable cross-brand comparison, while period-over-period analysis tracks growth trajectory and identifies high-velocity products driving platform revenue.

Tracks campaign and automation performance across consumer and professional brands, reporting on open rates, click-through rates, conversion rates, and unsubscribe trends. List health metrics including deliverability, bounce rates, and segmentation coverage provide a comprehensive view of eCRM database quality and audience engagement across both brand audiences.
